Stories from Korea

Forty years of life and ministry in Korea means forty years of stories. Some will appear in the autobiography. Some come back from those whose lives were touched. All are true.

Stories from Bill · From the Autobiography June 2, 1982

The Man Called Hama and the Suitcase at Gimpo Airport

I arrived at Gimpo International Airport late in the evening, June 2nd 1982. The man who had promised to meet me was not there yet. I came without any contact information in Seoul and I did not understand the culture engaged in "Korea Time." Before the smartphone and subway system, Korean people were always late. Traffic problems was the common excuse. I was stranded and I could not speak Korean and it seemed that nobody at the airport could speak English. All I could do was sit on one of my suitcases and worry about my unbearable problems.

That "late" man, nicknamed Hama (hippo), was one hour late. He must have been called Hama because for a Korean he had a rather large flat nose. He was supposed to take me to a simple motel for one night before putting me on a highway bus the next morning — south to Gwangju for my internship with Youth For Christ. It was scary because my mind was exploding. I thought I was going to have to teach English to 40 plus million Koreans — never realizing that common sense would tell me: Bill, you have to learn Korean.

Bill Ray Majors · First night in Korea · June 2, 1982
Letters from the Journey

Sometimes the fruit of ministry comes back across years — a message from someone whose life was touched. These letters remind us that the Word never returns void.

A Letter · 18 Years Later
Cassandra — IWE Young Nak Church, 2007

“Pastor Bill, when I lived in South Korea and attended your church, you once gave a sermon about Mary and Martha that resonated with me. Today, in my Lenten devotional, I was reminded of your sermon, as I saw the characters of Mary and Martha in a different event in their lives than you had preached on. Their character and their demeanor is the same in their stories, and as I journey I wonder if I have changed to be more like Mary and less like Martha.”

“This email is less about that question and more about the gratitude of your ministry to me in 2007. It is also a reminder to you and your family that today He sent you to my heart and to my prayers — and I think sometimes we all need to hear that. So if this finds you in need of a specific prayer request that you want to share, please do. If this feels awkward, as you probably don’t remember me, I hope you just feel the gratitude and joy that fills this message. Thank you.”

— Cassandra · Published with permission · A sermon preached in 2007, still speaking 18 years later
